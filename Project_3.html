Introduction

The passage discusses the introduction of a tool designed for efficient tracking and management of specimens in a busy Histology laboratory. The tool serves a dual purpose: informing management about the lab's current status and empowering staff to address outstanding specimens, thereby improving overall turnaround times. The lab currently faces extensive backlogs, especially in specimen reception and booking processes, leading to potential risks in specimen integrity and downstream molecular testing. The prolonged processing time poses a threat to patient diagnoses, particularly in cancer cases. The passage highlights the importance of implementing tracking measures to prevent delays, loss of specimens, and compromised integrity. It emphasizes the need for vigilant monitoring, facilitated by a visual aid, to ensure timely intervention and better service for patients.

Looking forward, the passage envisions the growing significance of the tool, especially with the advent of Digital Pathology and advanced tracking systems. Real-time tracking is expected to provide precise data on laboratory operations, enabling prompt corrective actions and maintaining efficient turnaround times. The tool, presented as a dashboard, will be accessible on various screens, including desktops and larger project screens for management presentations, with continuous display within the laboratory. The visual representation of crucial information is seen as a crucial element for the laboratory to regain control over specimen management.
